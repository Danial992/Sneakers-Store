About Sneakers

Sneakers, also known as athletic shoes, trainers, or tennis shoes, have become a staple in both fashion and sportswear over the past century. Originally designed for sports and physical activity, sneakers have evolved into versatile footwear suitable for casual, everyday use as well as competitive sports.

Sneakers trace their roots back to the late 19th century when rubber-soled shoes known as "plimsolls" were created for sports. These early versions lacked arch support but provided comfort and flexibility for walking and running. The term "sneaker" was coined because their rubber soles made them quieter than traditional hard-soled shoes, allowing wearers to "sneak" around.

In the 1920s, Converse introduced the Chuck Taylor All-Star, a shoe specifically designed for basketball, which quickly became iconic. In the decades that followed, companies like Adidas, Puma, and Nike entered the market, further popularizing sneakers with technological innovations and stylish designs.
